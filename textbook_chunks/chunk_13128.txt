Diagnosis is difficult and frequently missed because
symptoms of fibromyalgia are vague and generalized.
Coexisting nerve and muscle disorders such as rheuma-
toid arthritis, spinal arthritis, or Lyme disease may fur-
ther complicate the diagnostic process. Presently, there are
no tests available to specifically diagnose fibromyalgia.
The diagnosis is usually made after ruling out other med-
ical conditions with similar symptoms.
