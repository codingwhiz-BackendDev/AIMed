Endoscopy—Examination of body organs or cavities
through the use of an endoscope (a lighted optical
instrument used to see inside body cavities), such as
a cystoscope used to complete a cystoscopy.
Retrograde pyelogram—A pyelography or x-ray
technique where radiopaque dye is injected into
the kidneys from below, by way of the ureters,
allowing further examination of the kidneys.
Ureter—The tube that carries urine from the kidney
to the bladder, with each kidney having one ureter.
Urethra—A passageway from the bladder to the
outside for the discharge of urine. In the female,
this canal lies between the vagina and clitoris; in
the male, the urethra travels through the penis,
opening at the tip.
