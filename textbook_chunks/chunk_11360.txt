Acute phase reactant—A substance in the blood
that increases as a response to an acute conditions
such as infection, injury, tissue destruction, some
cancers, burns, surgery, or trauma.
Erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR)—The dis-
tance that red blood cells settle in a tube of blood
in one hour. It is an indication of inflammation.
Rouleaux—The stacking up of red blood cells,
caused by extra or abnormal proteins in the blood
that decrease the normal distance red cells main-
tain between each other.
