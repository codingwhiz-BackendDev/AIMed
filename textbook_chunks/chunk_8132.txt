There are many things that a diabetic individual can
do to prevent the occurrence of foot infections, including
the following:
• control blood glucose and do not allow it to get too high
• avoid smoking
• keep blood pressure and cholesterol under control
• exercise to stimulate blood flow
• keep feet clean, dry, and warm
• check your feet every day for blisters, scratches, and
skin that is hard, broken, inflamed or that feels hot or
cold when touched
• after bathing, carefully dry feet and apply thin coat of
petroleum jelly or hand cream to prevent dry skin from
cracking
• use a pumice stone and emery board to trim calluses
• do not neglect an ulcer, should one develop
