Gas embolism—The presence of a gas bubble in
the bloodstream that obstructs circulation.
Hyperbaric chamber—A sealed compartment in
which air pressure is gradually increased and then
gradually decreased, allowing nitrogen bubbles to
shrink and the nitrogen to safely diffuse out of
body tissue.
Lymphatic vessels—Vessels that carry a fluid
called lymph from the tissues to the bloodstream.
Nitrogen narcosis—Also called “rapture of the
deep,” the condition is caused by increased nitro-
gen pressure at depth and is characterized by
symptoms similar to alcohol intoxication.
