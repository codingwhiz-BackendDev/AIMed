Certain lifestyle changes may help reduce the work-
load on the heart and relieve symptoms. Some patients
may need to change their diet, stop drinking alcohol,
begin a physician-supervised exercise program, and/or
stop smoking.
Severe congestive cardiomyopathy usually causes
heart failure. When the heart muscle is damaged so
severely that medicines cannot help, a heart transplant
may be the only remaining treatment to be considered.
