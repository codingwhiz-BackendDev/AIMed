Mild and severe hemophilia A are inherited through
a complex genetic system that passes a recessive gene on
the female chromosome. Women usually do not show
signs of hemophilia but are carriers of the disease. Each
male child of the carrier has a 50% chance of having
hemophilia, and each female child has a 50% chance of
passing the gene on.
