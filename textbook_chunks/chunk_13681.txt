Escherichia coli (E. coli)
There are many strains of E. coli, and not all of them
are harmful. The strain that causes most severe food poi-
soning is E. coli O157:H7. Food poisoning by E. coli
occurs in three out of every 10,000 people. Foodborne E.
coli is found and transmitted mainly in food derived from
cows such as raw milk, raw or rare ground beef and fruit
or vegetables that are contaminated.
