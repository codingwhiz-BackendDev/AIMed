E. granulosus is found in many areas of Africa,
China, South America, Australia, New Zealand, and
Mediterranean and eastern Europe, as well as in parts of
the western United States. The parasite lives in regions
where dogs and livestock cohabitate. Direct exposure to
infectious dogs as well as parasitic eggs released into the
environment during shedding are both sources of human
infection.
In humans, cysts containing the larvae develop after
ingestion of eggs. Cysts form primarily in the lungs and
liver. Cysts developing in the liver are responsible for
about two-thirds of echinococcosis cases. Echinococco-
