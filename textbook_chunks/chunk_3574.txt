Choriocarcinomas result from genetic damage to a
germ cell. Males with Klinefelter syndrome are espe-
cially likely to develop extragonadal germ cell tumors.
The symptoms of a choriocarcinoma vary, depend-
ing on where the tumor originates and where it spreads.
In the uterus, the most common symptom is bleeding.
