Atresia—Failure to develop; complete absence.
Contrast agent—A substance that produces shad-
ows on an x ray so that hollow structures can be
more easily seen.
Gastroscopy—Looking into the stomach with a
flexible viewing instrument called a gastroscope.
Hypoplasia—Incomplete development.
Peptic ulcer—A wound in the lower stomach and
duodenum caused by stomach acid and a newly
discovered germ called Helicobacter pylori.
