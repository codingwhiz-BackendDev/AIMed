Cochlea—The hearing part of the inner ear. This
snail-shaped structure contains fluid and thou-
sands of microscopic hair cells tuned to various
frequencies.
Hair cells—Sensory receptors in the inner ear that
transform sound vibrations into messages that trav-
el to the brain.
Inner ear—The interior section of the ear, where
sound vibrations and information about balance
are translated into nerve impulses.
Middle ear—The small cavity between the
eardrum and the oval window that houses the
three tiny bones of hearing.
