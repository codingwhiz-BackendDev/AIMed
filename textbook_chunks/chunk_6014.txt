Increased levels of cortisol are associated with preg-
nancy. Physical and emotional stress can also elevate
cortisol levels. Drugs that may cause increased levels of
cortisol include estrogen, oral contraceptives, ampheta-
mines, cortisone, and spironolactone (Aldactone). Drugs
that may cause decreased levels include androgens,
aminoglutethimide, betamethasone, and other steroid
medications, danazol, lithium, levodopa, metyrapone and
phenytoin (Dilantin).
