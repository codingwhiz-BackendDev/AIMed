Surgical procedures for emphysema are very rare.
They are expensive and often not covered by insurance.
The great majority of patients cannot be helped by
surgery, and no single procedure is ideal for those who
can be helped. In January of 1996, the government tem-
porarily suspended Medicare payments for lung reduc-
tion surgery.
• Lung transplantation. Lung transplantation has been
successfully employed in some patients with end-stage
COPD. In the hands of an experienced team, the one-
year survival rate is over 70%.
