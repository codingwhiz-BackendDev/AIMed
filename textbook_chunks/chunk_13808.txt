Using common sense and following safety precau-
tions are the best ways to prevent foreign objects from
entering the body. For instance, parents and grandparents
should toddler-proof their homes, storing batteries in a
locked cabinet and properly disposing of used batteries,
so they are not in a location where curious preschoolers
can fish them out of a wastebasket. To minimize the
chance of youngsters inhaling food, parents should not
allow children to eat while walking or playing. Adults
should chew food thoroughly and not talk while chew-
ing. Many eye injuries can be prevented by wearing safe-
ty glasses while using tools
