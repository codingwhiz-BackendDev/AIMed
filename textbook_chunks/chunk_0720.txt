Improper or incomplete food preparation is the most
common way the disease is spread, with poultry account-
ing for over half the cases. Untreated water and raw milk
are also potential sources.
The incubation period after exposure is from one to
10 days. A day or two of mild fever, muscle aches, and
headache occur before intestinal symptoms begin. Diar-
rhea with or without blood and severe abdominal cramps
are the major intestinal symptoms. The severity of symp-
toms is variable, ranging from only mild fever to dehy-
dration and rarely death (mainly in the very young or
