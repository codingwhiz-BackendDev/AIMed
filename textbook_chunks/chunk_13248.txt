Prognosis depends on the extent of traumatic dam-
age to the affected area. Nail lacerations that are not
treated may cause nail deformities. When amputation is
accompanied with loss of two-thirds of the nail, half of
the fingers develop beaking, or a curved nail. Aftercare
and follow up are important components of treatment.
The patient is advised to keep the hand elevated, check
with a clinician two days after treatment, and to splint
fractures for two weeks in the extended position. Usually
a nail takes about 100 days to fully grow. Healing for an
