Any disease that changes the shape and size of red
blood cells decreases the ESR. Distorted cells, such as
with sickle cell disease, do not stack, and consequently
do not settle far, even in the presence of an ESR-associat-
ed disease. Diseases that cause the body to make less
protein or extra red blood cells also decrease the ESR.
