In about 95% of all cases of cholecystitis, the gall-
bladder contains gallstones. Gallstones are solid accu-
mulations of the components of bile, particularly choles-
terol, bile pigments, and calcium. These solids may
occur when the components of bile are not in the correct
proportion to each other. If the bile becomes overly con-
centrated, or if too much of one component is present,
stones may form. When these stones block the duct leav-
