HYPOCHLOREMIA. Hypochloremia usually occurs
as a result of sodium and potassium depletion (i.e.,
hyponatremia, hypokalemia). Severe depletion of serum
chloride levels causes metabolic alkalosis. This alkaliza-
tion of the bloodstream is characterized by:
• mental confusion
• slowed breathing
• paralysis
• muscle tension or spasm
