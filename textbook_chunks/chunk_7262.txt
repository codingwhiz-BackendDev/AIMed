Angiography—X rays of blood vessels filled with a
contrast agent.
Caloric testing—Flushing warm and cold water
into the ear stimulates the labyrinth and causes
vertigo and nystagmus if all the nerve pathways
are intact.
Electroencephalogram —Recording of electrical
activity in the brain.
Hospice—Systematized care of dying persons.
Living will—A legal document detailing a person’s
wishes during the end of life, to be carried out by
designated decision makers.
Stroke—Interruption of blood flow to a part of the
brain with consequent brain damage, also known
as a cerebrovascular accident (CVA).
