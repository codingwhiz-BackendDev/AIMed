A woman should receive regular prenatal care and
be able to alert her doctor to the first signs of trouble.
Once labor begins, she should be encouraged to move
around and to urinate. The doctor should be conservative
in diagnosing dystocia (nonprogressive labor) and fetal
distress, taking a position of “watchful waiting” before
deciding to operate.
