Achalasia—An esophageal disease of unknown
cause, in which the lower sphincter or muscle is
unable to relax normally, and leads to the accu-
mulation of material within the esophagus.
Endoscopy—Exam using an endoscope (a thin
flexible tube which uses a lens or miniature cam-
era to view various areas of the gastrointestinal
tract). When the procedure is performed to exam-
ine certain organs such as the bile ducts or pan-
creas, the organs are not viewed directly, but
rather indirectly through the injection of x ray.
Parasite—An organism that lives on or in another
and takes nourishment (food and fluids) from that
organism.
Regurgitation—Flow of material back up the
esophagus and into the throat or lungs.
