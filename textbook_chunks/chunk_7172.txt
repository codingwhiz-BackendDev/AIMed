Antibody—A special protein built by the body as a
defense against foreign material entering the body.
Cytomegalovirus (CMV)—A common human
virus causing mild or no symptoms in healthy peo-
ple, but permanent damage or death to an infect-
ed fetus, a transplant patient, or a person with HIV.
Titer—A dilution of a substance with an exact
known amount of fluid. For example, one part of
serum diluted with four parts of saline is a titer
of 1:4.
