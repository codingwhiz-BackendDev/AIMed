Antibiotics—Substances used against microorgan-
isms that cause infection.
Cataract—Clouding of the lens of the eye or its
capsule (surrounding membrane).
Computed tomography scan (CT scan)—A
process that uses x rays to create three-dimension-
al images of structures inside the body.
Electrolytes—Substances that conduct electric
current within the body and are essential for sus-
taining life.
Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)—The use of
electromagnetic energy to create images of struc-
tures inside the body.
Skin grafting—A technique in which a piece of
healthy skin from the patient’s body (or a donor’s)
is used to cover another part of the patient’s body
that has lost its skin.
